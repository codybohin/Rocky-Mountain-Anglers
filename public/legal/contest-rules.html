Official Rules

Contest Rules

⚠️ Draft — Requires Legal Review Before Launch

1. Nature of Competition

Rocky Mountain Anglers leaderboard competitions are skill-based contests, not games of chance. Rankings are determined entirely by verified fish length — a skill-based outcome dependent on angling ability, knowledge, and technique.

This is not a lottery. Entry fees are paid in exchange for leaderboard participation and access to the prize pot. Winners are determined by measurable, objective criteria (fish length as verified by AI-assisted photo review and community oversight).

2. Eligibility

✓ Must be 18 years of age or older (or the legal age of majority in your state, whichever is higher).

✓ Must hold a valid fishing license in the state where the catch was made.

✓ Must have purchased a Certified Angler Kit and received your RMA sticker.

✓ Must submit a photo clearly showing the RMA sticker, full ruler, fish head, and fish tail.

✗ Employees and immediate family members of Rocky Mountain Anglers are not eligible to compete.

✗ This platform is only available to residents of the United States. International participants are not currently eligible.

3. Prize Pot Structure

Entry Fee
$5.00 per catch
Platform Fee
5% of gross entries
First Place
60% of prize pot
Second Place
30% of prize pot
Third Place
10% of prize pot
Total Distribution
100% of prize pot

Prize pot = gross entry fees × 95%. Platform fee (5%) covers operating costs, verification infrastructure, and payment processing. 60% + 30% + 10% = 100% of the available prize pot.

4. State Availability

Rocky Mountain Anglers is designed to operate across Utah, Colorado, Wyoming, Montana, and Idaho. However, skill-based contest laws vary by state. This platform has not yet been reviewed by legal counsel in each state for compliance with applicable laws.

⚠️ Important Notice: Prize pot competitions are not currently accepting paid entries until state-by-state legal review is complete. The kit, fish diary, leaderboard, and reports are available. Paid submissions will be enabled after legal review.

5. Verification and Anti-Fraud

All leaderboard submissions go through AI-assisted photo verification. The system checks for:

• RMA sticker presence and readability

• Species identification

• Measurement plausibility vs. visible ruler

• Image authenticity indicators

Community members may flag suspicious catches. Three flags trigger a manual review by Rocky Mountain Anglers staff. Catches found to be fraudulent will be disqualified and the account banned. Intentional fraud forfeits the entry fee with no refund.

6. Prize Payouts

Prizes are paid via Stripe to the winner's verified payment method. Prize payouts exceeding $600 in a calendar year will require a completed W-9 form and will be reported to the IRS on Form 1099-MISC as required by federal law. Rocky Mountain Anglers is not responsible for taxes owed on prize winnings.
